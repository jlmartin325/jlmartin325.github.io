Morristown, TN Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Morristown Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 63,635 in 1970 to 113,953 in 2010. That was an increase of 79.1 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 85 in 1970 to 5,551 in 2010. That was an increase of 6430.6 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 0.1 percent to 4.9 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 70.6 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Natural change (B-D) was the primary factor in added population in the first period, replaced by NDM in the two most recent periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, the relative position of the factors adding the most population was unchanged.
