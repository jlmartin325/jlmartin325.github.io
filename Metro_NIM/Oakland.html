Oakland-Hayward-Berkeley, CA Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Oakland-Hayward-Berkeley Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 1,631,573 in 1970 to 2,559,296 in 2010. That was an increase of 56.9 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 112,692 in 1970 to 712,794 in 2010. That was an increase of 532.5 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 6.9 percent to 27.9 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 21.6 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residueal "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, net immigration (NIM) became the the major factor in population increase in all periods.
