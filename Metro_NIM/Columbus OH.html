Columbus, OH Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Columbus Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/decreased from 2,321,037 in 1970 to 2,077,240 in 2010. That was a decrease of 10.5 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population decreased from 151,064 in 1970 to 122,204 in 2010. That was a decrease of 19.1 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 6.5 percent to 5.9 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) decreased by 9.9 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
