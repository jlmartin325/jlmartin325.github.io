Visalia-Porterville, CA Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Visalia-Porterville Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 188,322 in 1970 to 442,179 in 2010. That was an increase of 134.8 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 12,021 in 1970 to 104,379 in 2010. That was an increase of 768.3 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 6.4 percent to 23.6 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 91.6 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
