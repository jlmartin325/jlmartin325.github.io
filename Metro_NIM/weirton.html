Weirton-Steubenville, WV-OH Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Weirton-Steubenville Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ decreased from 165,627 in 1970 to 124,454 in 2010. That was a decrease of 24.9 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population decreased from 6,368 in 1970 to 1,345 in 2010. That was a decrease of 78.9 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 3.8 percent to 1.1 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) decreased by 22.7 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. International migration (NIM) was the only factor that was not negative, and then only slightly in the two most recent periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NIM was the sole factor adding population in all periods as the MSA lost population overall.
