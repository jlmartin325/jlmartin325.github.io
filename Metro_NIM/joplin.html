Joplin, MO Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Joplin Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 112,753 in 1970 to 175,518 in 2010. That was an increase of 55.7 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 437 in 1970 to 6,380 in 2010. That was an increase of 1360.0 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 0.4 percent to 3.6 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 50.6 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Domestic migration (NDM) was the main source of added population in the first two periods, replaced by B-D in the latest period.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, the same pattern among the three factors was maintained.
