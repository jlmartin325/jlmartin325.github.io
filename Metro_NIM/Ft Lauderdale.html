Fort Lauderdale-Pompano Beach-Deerfield Beach Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Fort Lauderdale-Pompano Beach-Deerfield Beach Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 620,100 in 1970 to 1,748,066 in 2010. That was an increase of 181.9 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 49,604 in 1970 to 558,399 in 2010. That was an increase of 1025.7 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 8.0 percent to 31.9 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 108.5 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residueal "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Domestic migration (NDM) was the principal source of population increase in the first period, and it was replaced in the next two periods by NIM.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, net immigration (NIM) became a larger factor in population increase. It was the sole factor adding population in the second period and most of the increase in population in the most recent period.
