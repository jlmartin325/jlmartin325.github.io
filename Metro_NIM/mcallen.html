McAllen-Edinburg-Mission, TX Metro Area Immigration

Population

The McAllen-Edinburg-Mission Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 181,535 in 1970 to 774,769 in 2010. That was an increase of 326.8 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 22,845 in 1970 to 225,207 in 2010. That was an increase of 885.8 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 12.6 percent to 29.1 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 246.3 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
