Yuba City, CA Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Yuba City Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1 increased from 41,935 in 1970 to 94,737 in 2010. That was an increase of 125.9 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 2,595 in 1970 to 21,930 in 2010. That was an increase of 745.1 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 6.2 percent to 23.1 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 85.1 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
