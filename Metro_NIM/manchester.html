Manchester-Nashua, NH Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Manchester-Nashua Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/increased from 223,941 in 1970 to 400,721 in 2010. That was an increase of 78.9 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 16,197 in 1970 to 33,320 in 2010. That was an increase of 105.7 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 7.2 percent to 8.3 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 76.9 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Natural change (B-D) was the primary source of added population in all periods.
