Camden, NJ Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Camden, NJ Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 952,104 in 1970 to 1,250,679 in 2010. That was an increase of 31.4 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 35,767 in 1970 to 108,354 in 2010. That was an increase of 202.9 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 8.8 percent to 18.0 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 24.7 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residueal "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Natural change (B-D) was the largest factor accounting for population growth in all periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, B-D remained the principal contributor to population increase in the first period, and NIM was the principal factor increasing population in the two most recent periods.
