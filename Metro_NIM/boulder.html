Boulder, CO Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Boulder Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 66,870 in 1970 to 97,385 in 2010. That was an increase of 45.6 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 2,430 in 1970 to 11,220 in 2010. That was an increase of 361.7 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 3.6 percent to 11.5 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 33.7 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Domestic migration (NDM) was the primary factor in population increase in the first and third period and B-D held that position in the second period.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NIM switched positions with B-D as the major source of population increase in the second period as NDM became negative.
