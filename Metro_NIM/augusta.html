Augusta-Richmond County, GA-SC Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Augusta-Richmond County Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 398,938 in 1970 to 1,716,289 in 2010. That was an increase of 330.2 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 11,943 in 1970 to 242,400 in 2010. That was an increase of 1929.6 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 3.0 percent to 14.1 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 280.9 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Natural change (B-D) was the principal factor adding population in all periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, B-D remained the primary factor in population increase in the first period, while NIM became the primary factor in the two most recent periods.
