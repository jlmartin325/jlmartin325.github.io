Cambridge-Newton-Framingham, MA Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Cambridge-Newton-Framingham, MA Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 2,035,155 in 1970 to 2,246,244 in 2010. That was an increase of 10.4 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 179,899 in 1970 to 404,433 in 2010. That was an increase of 124.8 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 8.8 percent to 18.0 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) decreased by 0.7 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residueal "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Natural change (B-D) was the largest factor accounting for population growth in the first and NIM was the principal factor in the most recent two periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, net immigration (NIM) was the principal factor in population increase in all periods and it rose to 93.7 percent of the overall population increase in the 2000-09 period.
