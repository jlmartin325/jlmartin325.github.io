Kokomo, IN Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Kokomo Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ decreased from 83,198 in 1970 to 82,752 in 2010. That was a decrease of 0.5 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 741 in 1970 to 1,568 in 2010. That was an increase of 111.6 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 0.9 percent to 1.9 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) decreased by 1.5 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. In it, natural change (B-D) was the primary factor adding population in all periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NDM remained the primary factor adding population except for the most recent period when NIM added a larger share.
