Raleigh, NC Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Raleigh Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 317,010 in 1970 to 1,130,490 in 2010. That was an increase of 256.6 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 2,959 in 1970 to 130,833 in 2010. That was an increase of 4321.5 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 0.9 percent to 11.6 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 218.3 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Domestic migration (NDM) was the primary source of increased population in all periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NDM remained the primary source of population increase, but NIM became the second largest factor in the two most recent periods.
