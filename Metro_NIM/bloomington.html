Bloomington, IN Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Bloomington Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 168,508 in 1970 to 302,317 in 2010. That was an increase of 75.8 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 3,455 in 1970 to 15,972 in 2010. That was an increase of 362.3 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 2.0 percent to 5.3 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 69.9 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Natural change (B-D) was the primary source of population increase in the first two periods, replaced by NIM in the most recent period.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NIM also contributed the most to population increase in the second period as well as in the most recent period.
