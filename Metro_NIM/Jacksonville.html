Jacksonville, FL Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Jacksonville Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 621,519 in 1970 to 1,345,596 in 2010. That was an increase of 116.5 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 11,020 in 1970 to 105,071 in 2010. That was an increase of 853.5 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 1.8 percent to 7.0 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 103.2 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. In it, natural change (B-D) was the primary factor in population increase in the first and most recent periods. Domestic migration (NDM) was the principal factor in the second period.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NDM was the principal factor in population increase in the first two periods and was edged out by NIM in the most recent period.
