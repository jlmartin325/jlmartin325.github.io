Las Cruces, NM Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Las Cruces Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 91,724 in 1970 to 238,553 in 2010. That was an increase of 160.1 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 6,054 in 1970 to 38,629 in 2010. That was an increase of 538.1 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 6.6 percent to 16.2 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 133.4 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. In it, natural change (B-D) was the primary factor in population increase in all periods, and sole factor in the most recent period.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NIM replaced B-D as the principal factor adding population in all periods - including the most recent period despite the absence of an increase from net immigrant settlement.
