Michigan City-La Porte, IN Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Michigan City-La Porte Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/increased from 105,342 in 1970 to 111,467 in 2010. That was an increase of 5.8 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 2,607 in 1970 to 3,495 in 2010. That was an increase of 34.1 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 2.5 percent to 3.1 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 5.1 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
