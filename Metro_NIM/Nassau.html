Nassau-Suffolk, NY Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Nassau-Suffolk Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/increased from 2,553,030 in 1970 to 2,832,882 in 2010. That was an increase of 11.0 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 192,800 in 1970 to 499,786 in 2010. That was an increase of 159.2 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 7.6 percent to 17.6 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) decreased by 1.1 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residueal "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Natural change (B-D) was the primary factor in population increase in all periods, and NDM was consistantly negative.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NIM became the principal factor in population increase and was the sole source of SSWS POPULtion in the most recent period.
