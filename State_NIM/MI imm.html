Michigan State Immigration

Population

Michigan's population increased from 7,823,570 in 1960 to 9,877,574 in 2010. That was an increase of 26.3 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 529,624 in 1960 to 587,747 in 2010. That was an increase of 11.0 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 6.8 percent to 5.9 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 27.5 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
