Pennsylvania State Immigration

Population

Pennsylvania's population increased from 11,319,366 in 1960 to 12,709,630 in 2010. That was an increase of 12.3 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 603,490 in 1960 to 739,068 in 2010. That was an increase of 22.5 percent. The foreign-born share increased from 5.3 percent to 5.8 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 11.6 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Domestic migration (NDM) was consistently negative and the principal factor adding population changed from B-D in the first period to NIM in the latest two periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NIM rose from being a bare majority of population increase in the unadjusted data to a large majority in the adjsuted data; rising to more than nine-tenths of the overall population increase in the most recent period.
