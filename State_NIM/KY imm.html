Kentucky State Immigration

Population

Kentucky's population increased from 3,038,156 in 1960 to 4,336,266 in 2010. That was an increase of 42.7 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 16,830 in 1960 to 140,583 in 2010. That was an increase of 735.3 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 0.6 percent to 3.2 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 39.0 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
