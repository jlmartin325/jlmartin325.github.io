Georgia State Immigration

Population

Georgia's population increased from 3,943,116 in 1960 to 9,712,587 in 2010. That was an increase of 146.3 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 25,300 in 1960 to 942,959 in 2010. That was an increase of 3627.1 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 0.6 percent to 9.7 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 123.2 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows NDM as the largest factor in population change in the first period, replaced by B-D in the two most recent periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NIM became nearly as large as B-D as the main factor in adding population.
