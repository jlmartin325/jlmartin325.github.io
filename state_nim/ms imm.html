Mississippi State Immigration

Population

Mississippi's population increased from 2,178,141 in 1960 to 2,990,036 in 2010. That was an increase of 36.2 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 8,058 in 1960 to 61,428 in 2010. That was an increase of 662.3 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 0.4 percent to 2.1 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 33.9 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
