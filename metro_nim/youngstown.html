Youngstown-Warren-Boardman, OH-PA Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Youngstown-Warren-Boardman Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1 increased from 233,678 in 1970 to 341,270 in 2010. That was an increase of 46.0 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 5,641 in 1970 to 8,476 in 2010. That was an increase of 50.3 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 2.4 percent to 2.5 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 45.9 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. In it, natural change (B-D) was the chief factor adding population in the first period, and NIM was the sole factor adding net population in the most recent two periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, the position of the population change factors was unchanged.
