Corvallis, OR Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Corvallis Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/increased from 53,776 in 1970 to 85,581 in 2010. That was an increase of 50.6 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 1,705 in 1970 to 7,171 in 2010. That was an increase of 320.6 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 3.2 percent to 8.4 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 50.6 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Natural change (B-D) was the principal factor in adding population in the earliest period, replaced by NIM in the two most recent periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, the population change factors kept the same relative positions.
