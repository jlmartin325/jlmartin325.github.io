Warren-Troy-Farmington Hills, MI Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Warren-Troy-Farmington Hills Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 1,764,639 in 1970 to 2,475,666 in 2010. That was an increase of 40.3 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 105,070 in 1970 to 229,113 in 2010. That was an increase of 118.1 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 6.0 percent to 9.3 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 35.4 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residueal "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Natural change (B-D) was the primary factor in population increase in the first two periods, replaced by NIM in the most recent period.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NIM was the primary factor in population increase in all periods.
