West Palm Beach-Boca Raton-Delray Beach, FL Metro Area Immigration

Population

The West Palm Beach-Boca Raton-Delray Beach Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 348,753 in 1970 to 1,320,134 in 2010. That was an increase of 278.5 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 27,384 in 1970 to 306,611 in 2010. That was an increase of 1019.7 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 7.9 percent to 23.2 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 215.4 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residueal "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Domestic migration (NDM) was the primary factor in population increase in all periods.
