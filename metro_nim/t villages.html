The Villages, FL Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Villages Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 14,839 in 1970 to 94,074 in 2010. That was an increase of 534.0 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 376 in 1970 to 4,002 in 2010. That was an increase of 964.4 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 0.6 percent to 5.2 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 29.6 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
