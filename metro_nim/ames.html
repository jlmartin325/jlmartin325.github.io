Ames, IA Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Ames, IA Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 62,783 in 1970 to 89,542 in 2010. That was an increase of 42.6 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 1,806 in 1970 to 7,061 in 2010. That was an increase of 291.0 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 2.9 percent to 7.9 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 35.3 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. There was a constant population outflow from NDM. Natural change (B-D) accounted for most of the population increase in the first two periods, and NIM had the largest share in the latest period.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, B-D accounted for about two-thirds of the population increase in the earliest period and NIM about one-third. In the most recent period that was reversed - NIM accounted for about two-thirds of the increase.
