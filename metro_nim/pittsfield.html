Pittsfield, MA Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Pittsfield Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ decreased from 149,402 in 1970 to 131,219 in 2010. That was a decrease of 12.2 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population decreased from 8,171 in 1970 to 6,447 in 2010. That was a decrease of 21.1 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 5.5 percent to 4.9 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) decreased by 11.7 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. International migration (NIM) was the main source of added population in the first period and the sole source in the two most recent periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, NIM was the sole source of added population in all periods.
