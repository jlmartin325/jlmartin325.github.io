Prescott, AZ Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Prescott Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 36,733 in 1970 to 211,033 in 2010. That was an increase of 474.5 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 1,199 in 1970 to 14,849 in 2010. That was an increase of 1138.4 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 3.3 percent to 7.0 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 452.1 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residueal "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
