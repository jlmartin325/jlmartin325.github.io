Newark-Union, NJ-PA Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Newark-Union Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 2,213,992 in 1970 to 2,471,171 in 2010. That was an increase of 11.6 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 206,255 in 1970 to 549,743 in 2010. That was an increase of 166.5 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 9.3 percent to 22.2 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) decreased by 4.3 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residueal "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. The major factor in population increase for the first two periods was B-D and it was NIM for the most recent period. NDM was negative in all periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, net immigration (NIM) became the the major factor in population increase in the first two periods and the sole factor in the most recent period.
