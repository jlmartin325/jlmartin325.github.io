East Stroudsburg ALL Metro Area Immigration

Population

The East Stroudsburg Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 45,604 in 1970 to 169,896 in 2010. That was an increase of 272.5 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 8,066 in 2000 to 16,541 in 2010. That was an increase of 105.1 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 5.8 percent to 9.7 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 16.4 percent from 2000 to 2010.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
