Sebastian-Vero Beach, FL Metro Area Immigration

Population

The Sebastian-Vero Beach Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 35,992 in 1970 to 138,026 in 2010. That was an increase of 283.5 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 1,605 in 1970 to 14,229 in 2010. That was an increase of 786.5 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 4.5 percent to 10.3 percent.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 260.0 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.
