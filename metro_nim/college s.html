College Station-Bryan, TX Metro Area Immigration

Population

The College Station-Bryan Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) population 1/ increased from 82,336 in 1970 to 228,660 in 2010. That was an inecrease of 177.6 percent.

The foreign-born (FB) population increased from 1,895 in 1970 to 25,471 in 2010. That was an increase of 1244.1 percent. The foreign-born share changed from 2.3 percent to 11.1 percet.

The share of the overall population that was native-born (NB) increased by 152.5 percent.

Sources of Population Change

The primary sources of population change are:
  • Natural Change [Births minus Deaths or B-D];
  • Net Domestic Migration [NDM - movement between states]; and
  • Net International Migration [NIM - movement into and out of the country].
  • There is also a residual "other" amount that isn't placed in one of those three categories.

The first chart below shows the three population change factors for three periods adjusted for annual average amounts. Natural change (B-D) was the primary source of population increase in all periods.

The second chart shows the same data but with an adjustment to reflect births to immigrants shifted to NIM. In it, B-D remained the primary factor in population increase in the earliest period but was supplanted by NIM in the two most recent periods.
